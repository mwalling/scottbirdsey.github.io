Independent, part 1

Presently I’m through with Facebook. This morning I found someone sharing a YouTube video of a man with a green screen purporting to be the “virtual president”, giving a “speech” about crime control, and the dangers of believing the political agenda of gun control. Interspersed through the video was footage of the US Congress in the audience, listening with grave intensity. There’s a subtext, here – not one that is terribly new or unique. I guess it’s just the last straw.

In 2003, as a freshman at NYU, I watched others around me all joining Facebook. I declined; MySpace allowed for more creative expression and didn’t sequester whom I could speak to. Eventually I caved for reasons of ego and convenience.

This is my homestead, right here. It’s not much, not very convenient, and fairly out of the way. That’s fine. I’m going for a run in the foothills of Silver City, now.
